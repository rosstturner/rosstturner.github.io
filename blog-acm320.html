Mar 5 2021

Second Camera

I took the advice of my professor and thought about how to add a second camera. Since the room is so small, I had a hard time trying to figure a different way to show the room and also how it would change/drive the story. I decided to add a small security camera that would add to the feeling that we are watching the main computer through the eyes of more technology. It took me a while to get the look how I wanted. I discovered that hidden within the Arnold settings of the camera, you can change the rotation to give it a convex look like a camera lens can do. I then took this image and edited it further in Photoshop but will add all the effects to the final render in After Effects. I really like the look and feel of the security camera and I think that it will be very useful to have another angle that I can then use to zoom in on the hand of my character when he is first discovering the color change effect.

Jan 20 2021

Testing Color Swipe Effect

I have been runing various test trying to figure out how to animated one color being taken over by another. Using the two different textures with a mix node and then using a ramp to control the mix, plus applying noise, keyframe the values of the ramp and show this swirl effect. I think this will be the best way to do simple color swaps while rendering. I have also started looking into After Effects to see if I can just render the entire film in black and white and then again in color. I could then use After Effects and masking to handle all the color swaps in post and also come up with some neat tracking color swaps so that the robot can do more then just instant change the color of something. Just an idea right now.
Jan 18 2021

Final Character Design and Shader

Designing out the robot was pretty simple. He is made of of very basic shapes and should be very easy to rig. Two problem areas with this design are going to be how to animate the wheels going around when they are an odd shape and the face design moving with the body. The face is unique because it is made from a MASH node, MASH nodes are super useful but I am not sure how they work with animating. I have completed test in the past for animating a face in MASH but never having the entire MASH network move along with a character. Finally, I needed to work on the style for my film. I took inspiration from the latest Spiderman and decided on a Halftone Toon Shader texture. This was not as easy as the modeling phase as I had to build the entire texture from scratch. There is a built in Halftone texture in Maya but sadly will not work with Arnold and it also did not look as nice as what I was able to achieve in the end. I tied everything together in the end with a color ramp that allowed me to have the toon shader effect with different sized halftones depending on the color. I think this will work well for my film.
Jan 15 2021

ACM 320 Animated Film Concept

As a huge fan of Love, Death, and Robots, I wanted to make an animated film about some sort of robot. Taking inspiration from Wall-E, I thought of a dystopian world. I want it to be bland and without any color. I then doodled out what my little bot would look like and started to imagin how he would feel in this world. I then imagined him all of a sudden experiencing color for the first time and I decided that this could be the focus of my animated short. With a little help from my wife I talked through my story and we thought it would be cute if he turns into color for the first time when he gets shocked one day. Because of this shock, he wanted to bring color to the world and goes out on an adventure bringing turing the bland world into color. I am still thinking of a good ending but am leaning towards the robot stumnbling across some form of junction box and thinking that if he shocks it the whole city will be colorful. This of course ends poorly with everything being shut off leaving the world a dark and colorless place. Just his eyes are seen glowing at the end.
